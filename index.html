Biography

Jose Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (1861–1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary. Born in Calamba, Laguna, Rizal excelled in multiple fields, from literature and medicine to the arts. He is best known for his novels Noli Me Tángere and El Filibusterismo, which exposed social injustices under Spanish rule and galvanized the Philippine nationalist movement. His writings advocated for peaceful reform, education, and equality, ultimately inspiring a generation of Filipinos to strive for independence.

Executed by the Spanish in 1896, Rizal became a martyr whose life and works continue to influence the Philippines’ pursuit of self-determination and national identity. His essay The Philippines a Century Hence remains a cornerstone of Filipino political thought, anticipating both the trials and triumphs the nation would face on its path to freedom.
